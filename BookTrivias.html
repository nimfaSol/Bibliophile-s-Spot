Book Trivias

The longest novel ever written is "In Search of Lost Time" by Marcel Proust—over 1.2 million words!
Dr. Seuss wrote "Green Eggs and Ham" using only 50 different words.
Shakespeare invented over 1,700 words, including "bedroom," "eyeball," and "lonely."
The most stolen book from public libraries is the Guinness Book of World Records.
Don Quixote by Miguel de Cervantes is considered the first modern novel.
The first book ever printed using movable type was the Gutenberg Bible in the 1450s.
Agatha Christie is the best-selling fiction author of all time—her books have sold over 2 billion copies.
The Harry Potter series has been translated into over 80 languages, including Latin and Ancient Greek.
The Catcher in the Rye was once the most banned book in U.S. schools.
The original title of Pride and Prejudice was First Impressions.
Stephen King threw away his draft of Carrie—his wife dug it out and convinced him to finish it.
The first novel written on a typewriter was The Adventures of Tom Sawyer by Mark Twain.
The world's smallest book is 0.74 mm x 0.75 mm and can only be read with a microscope.
The Library of Congress is the largest library in the world with over 170 million items.
Neil Gaiman wrote Coraline for his daughter because she wanted a story about a girl who had adventures.
